Anatomy: Introduction to the structure of the human body, including gross anatomy, histology, and embryology. Emphasis on the study of organs, tissues, and their functions.

Physiology:Study of normal body functions and mechanisms, including cell biology, biochemistry, and molecular biology. Understanding the principles of homeostasis and organ systems.

Biochemistry: Exploration of biochemical processes occurring within living organisms, focusing on metabolism, enzyme kinetics, and molecular genetics. Introduction to carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids.

Biophysics: Basic principles of physics applied to biological systems, including thermodynamics, electricity, and optics. Understanding the physical basis of physiological processes.

Medical Ethics:Discussion of ethical principles and professional conduct in the medical field. Exploration of issues such as patient confidentiality, informed consent, and end-of-life care.

Introduction to Clinical Skills: Introduction to basic clinical skills such as patient communication, history-taking, and physical examination. Hands-on training in a simulated environment.

Medical Terminology:Introduction to medical terminology, including prefixes, suffixes, and root words. Development of skills in deciphering and interpreting medical terms.

Pathology: Study of the nature, causes, and effects of diseases, including cellular and molecular mechanisms. Exploration of general pathology and systemic pathology.

Microbiology: Introduction to microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. Emphasis on the principles of microbial pathogenesis, diagnosis, and treatment.

Pharmacology: Introduction to the principles of drug action, including pharmacodynamics, pharmacokinetics, and drug interactions. Study of major drug classes and their therapeutic uses.

Immunology: Exploration of the immune system, including innate and adaptive immunity, antigen recognition, and immune responses. Understanding of immunological disorders and vaccination.

Pathophysiology: Study of the physiological processes underlying disease states, including alterations in cellular function, tissue injury, and organ dysfunction. Integration of knowledge from anatomy, physiology, and biochemistry.

Medical Genetics: Introduction to the principles of genetics, including inheritance patterns, genetic disorders, and genetic counseling. Understanding of molecular genetics techniques and their applications in medicine.

Introduction to Clinical Medicine: Application of basic medical sciences to clinical practice, including patient evaluation, differential diagnosis, and management. Introduction to clinical reasoning and evidence-based medicine.

Internal Medicine: Rotation through various medical specialties, including cardiology, gastroenterology, pulmonology, nephrology, and endocrinology. Emphasis on patient assessment, diagnosis, and management.

Surgery: Rotation through surgical specialties, including general surgery, orthopedic surgery, neurosurgery, and urology. Exposure to surgical procedures, preoperative and postoperative care, and surgical emergencies.

Pediatrics: Rotation through pediatric specialties, including neonatology, pediatric cardiology, pediatric oncology, and pediatric infectious diseases. Emphasis on child development, growth, and common pediatric illnesses.

Obstetrics and Gynecology: Rotation through obstetrics and gynecology departments, including prenatal care, labor and delivery, gynecological surgery, and reproductive endocrinology. Exposure to women's health issues and maternal-fetal medicine.

Psychiatry: Rotation through psychiatric departments, including inpatient and outpatient settings. Exposure to psychiatric assessment, diagnosis, and management of mental health disorders.

Family Medicine: Rotation through primary care clinics, focusing on comprehensive healthcare for individuals and families. Emphasis on preventive medicine, health promotion, and continuity of care.

Elective Rotation: Opportunity for students to choose elective rotations in specialized areas of interest, such as radiology, dermatology, ophthalmology, or emergency medicine. Hands-on experience under the supervision of experienced clinicians.

Critical Care Medicine: Rotation through intensive care units, including medical, surgical, and neonatal intensive care. Emphasis on the management of critically ill patients, advanced life support, and interdisciplinary teamwork.

Emergency Medicine: Rotation through emergency departments, focusing on the assessment and management of acute medical and surgical emergencies. Exposure to trauma resuscitation, triage, and emergency procedures.

Neurology: Rotation through neurology departments, including stroke units, epilepsy clinics, and neurorehabilitation centers. Emphasis on the diagnosis and management of neurological disorders, including stroke, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Ophthalmology: Rotation through ophthalmology clinics, focusing on the diagnosis and management of eye diseases and disorders. Exposure to ophthalmic examinations, diagnostic techniques, and surgical procedures

Ear, Nose, and Throat (ENT): Rotation through ENT clinics, including otolaryngology, audiology, and speech therapy. Emphasis on the diagnosis and management of ear, nose, and throat disorders, including hearing loss, sinusitis, and tonsillitis.

Community Medicine: Rotation through community health centers, focusing on public health initiatives, epidemiological studies, and health promotion campaigns. Participation in community outreach programs and health education activities.

Research Elective: Opportunity for students to engage in research projects under the guidance of faculty mentors. Conducting literature reviews, data collection, analysis, and presentation of research findings.

Internship Rotation: Completion of a mandatory internship rotation in a clinical setting, providing supervised practice experience in various medical specialties. Emphasis on clinical decision-making, patient management, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Residency Preparation: Guidance and support for students preparing to apply for residency programs in their chosen specialty. Assistance with residency application process, including preparing curriculum vitae, personal statements, and letters of recommendation.

Clinical Skills Refinement: Continued development and refinement of clinical skills, including physical examination techniques, diagnostic reasoning, and patient communication. Preparation for the transition to postgraduate medical training.

Professional Development: Exploration of professional roles and responsibilities, including ethical principles, legal obligations, and professional conduct. Preparation for licensure examinations and certification requirements.

Capstone Project: Completion of a capstone project or thesis on a topic of interest within the field of medicine. Independent research, data analysis, and presentation of findings to faculty and peers.

Clinical Rotations: Optional elective rotations in specialized areas of medicine to further enhance clinical skills and knowledge. Opportunity for hands-on experience in advanced medical procedures and techniques.

Career Guidance: Career Guidance: Guidance and support for students transitioning from medical school to residency training or clinical practice. Exploration of career pathways, fellowship opportunities, and professional networking.
